Cox's Bazar: Queen of natural beauty

Cox's Bazar is situated 152km south of Chittagong city. Its distance from Dhaka is 414 km. It is the largest tourism center in Bangladesh.

In coxbazar, the world’s largest sandy beach is not only for worth visiting but also a lot of places can daze you by their beauty, rarity and uniqueness. There are several most famous sea beach point which are the most crowded area and peoples are usually goes there.

Jaflong - A place of great natural beauty

Jaflong is a natural tourist spot in the Division of Sylhet, Bangladesh. It’s about 60 km far from Sylhet town and takes two hours drive to reach there. It is located in Gowainghat Upazila of Sylhet District and situated at the border between Bangladesh and the Indian state of Meghalaya.

Jaflong is one of the most adventurous tourist spots in Sylhet division. It contains awesome natural beauty. Visitors also see an enormous beautiful waterfall on the way of Sylhet to Jaflong. Jaflong is totally a hilly area of real natural beauty where hills are greenish with the forests. You can see the lifestyle of Tribe Khasia in Jaflong. Zero point of Jaflong is located in this river and it is very much attractive.

Kuakata – Daughter Of The Sea In The East Of Bangladesh

Kuakata Beach is a beach situated in Kuakata, Patuakhali District, Bangladesh. Kuakata beach is the second largest sea beach in Bangladesh

Kuakata is a natural beauty, with the silent sandy beach, making it a perfect destination for travelers who prefer a calm environment. The sun rising and setting in the Bay of Bengal makes it a unique place in this region.

Sajek Valley: Beauty Queen of Bangladesh

The valley is situated among the hills of Kasalong range of mountains in Sajek union, Baghaichhari Upazila in Rangamati District; precisely it is 67 km north-east of Khagrachhari town and 95 km North-West of Rangamati city.

Sajek valley is famous for its natural beauty. Sajek valley is known as the Queen of Hills & Roof of Rangamati. The valley is surrounded by mountains, dense forest, grasslands hilly tracks. Many small rivers flow through the mountains among which Kachalong and Machalong are notable. The road to Sajek has high picks and falls.
